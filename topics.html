Topic Description

The abdomen is the part of the body between the thorax (chest) and pelvis. It contains most of the tubular gastrointestinal tract as well as several solid organs. Understanding abdominal anatomy is crucial for clinical examination, surgical procedures, and diagnostic imaging.

Key structures include the abdominal wall, peritoneal cavity, gastrointestinal organs (stomach, small and large intestines), liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, kidneys, and major blood vessels. This topic covers surface anatomy, regional anatomy, cross-sectional anatomy, and clinical correlations.

Learning Objectives

  • Describe the layers of the anterior abdominal wall and their clinical significance
  • Identify the organs within the abdominal cavity and their anatomical relationships
  • Understand the peritoneal reflections and their importance in abdominal pathology
  • Trace the course of major blood vessels and nerves in the abdomen
  • Correlate anatomical knowledge with common clinical conditions and procedures
