Chapter Description

Embryology is the branch of biology that studies the prenatal development of gametes, fertilization, and development of embryos and fetuses. This chapter covers human development from zygote to fetus, including all critical stages of organogenesis and developmental abnormalities.

Key areas include gametogenesis, fertilization, implantation, embryonic folding, development of organ systems, fetal membranes, and clinical correlations with congenital anomalies. Understanding embryology is crucial for comprehending adult anatomy and many pathological conditions.

Learning Objectives

  • Describe the process of gametogenesis and early embryonic development
  • Understand the formation of germ layers and their derivatives
  • Trace the development of major organ systems
  • Identify critical periods of development and potential teratogenic effects
  • Correlate embryological development with congenital anomalies
